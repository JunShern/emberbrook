# Dialogue Calibration Sample

Two high-traffic stretches rewritten under `docs/VOICES.md`. BEFORE lines are verbatim
from code; AFTER lines are the target register. Same story beats, same lore facts, same
emotional peaks — radically more readable.

Conventions used below:
- **(kept)** — the line already passes the bible and ships unchanged. The rewrite pass is
  surgical, not scorched-earth: a line that is already plain, in-voice, and short survives.
- **→ two boxes** — one overlong BEFORE line becomes two dialogue boxes.
- Notes in *italics* explain the load-bearing choices for the rewrite agents.

---

## SAMPLE A — Chapter 1: Poppy at the festival
`chapter1.js`, `talkTo('poppy')`, pre-Hush. Both sub-branches covered.

*This is the game's first lore explanation (the Kindling Hour) and the player's first
taste of Poppy. Per STORY.md §7 the explanation is plain knowledge — warm, proud,
matter-of-fact, never mysterious — and must preserve all four facts: yearly telling ·
teller keeps the memory · flame keeps the felt part · shines it back through the lamps on
everyone in the light.*

### A1 — Vesper's visit (11 lines → 13 boxes)

**1. POPPY**
- BEFORE: "A new face! Nobody passes through Emberbrook on Emberwake without eating something warm. That is not hospitality, that is law."
- AFTER: "A new face! Here, take a bun, love. Nobody goes hungry in Emberbrook on Emberwake — that's not kindness, that's LAW."

*Command first ("take a bun") — Poppy leads with the action, then legislates it. "That is
not hospitality" was explaining the joke; "that's not kindness, that's LAW" says it faster.*

**2. VESPER**
- BEFORE: "I— alright. One. For the record, I'm here to chart the Whisperwood, not to eat pastry."
- AFTER: "I— fine. One. For the record: I'm here to chart the Whisperwood, not to eat pastry."

*Nearly kept. "For the record:" with the colon is her list-rhythm; "alright"→"fine" is
one syllable of extra dryness.*

**3. POPPY (laughing)**
- BEFORE: "Chart the— HA! Did you hear her? Eat two."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "Chart the— HA! Did you hear her? Eat two."

*Already perfect: seven words, plain, in-voice, and "Eat two." is her signature tic.
This is the model the rest of the scene is being pulled toward.*

**4. POPPY**
- BEFORE: "And where's home for you, love? Everybody's road starts somewhere."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "And where's home for you, love? Everybody's road starts somewhere."

**5. VESPER**
- BEFORE: "Somewhere is under review. I walk routes for a living — ask me again when the survey's done."
- AFTER: "Nowhere, yet. It's under survey. Ask me again when the survey's done."

*"Somewhere is under review" made the player parse a joke before the meaning. "Nowhere,
yet" answers the question first (plain first), then the deflection lands as her voice.*

**6. VESPER**
- BEFORE: "Fine. Question, though — everyone keeps saying 'the Kindling Hour' like I was born knowing what it is."
- AFTER: "My turn for a question. Everyone keeps saying 'the Kindling Hour' like I was born knowing what it is."

*Light trim; the setup was already doing its job.*

**7. POPPY** → two boxes
- BEFORE: "Simplest thing in the world, love. Once a year you bring your best memory and TELL it to the flame. You keep the memory — the flame keeps the WARM of it, and shines it back on every soul in the lamplight, forever."
- AFTER (box 1): "Simplest thing in the world, love. Once a year you bring your best memory and TELL it to the flame."
- AFTER (box 2): "You keep the memory. The flame keeps the warmth of it — and shines it back on every soul in the lamplight, forever."

*Lore-critical: all four facts preserved, wording kept close to canon phrasing. The only
real change is the split — 43 words was over ceiling for one box. One capped word per box
(rule 8): TELL keeps its cap, WARM loses its.*

**8. VESPER**
- BEFORE: "You give your memories. To a fire. On purpose."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "You give your memories. To a fire. On purpose."

*Eight words, three beats, pure Vesper. Untouchable.*

**9. POPPY (happy)** → two boxes
- BEFORE: "GIVE? Tell! You lose nothing, love — every minute stays yours. The flame takes what it MEANT, and pays it back to all of us, all year. Why do you think nobody in Emberbrook stays angry past sundown?"
- AFTER (box 1): "Give? TELL, love. You lose nothing — you keep every minute. The flame only takes what it meant to you."
- AFTER (box 2): "And it pays that back to all of us, all year. Why do you think nobody here stays angry past sundown?"

*The keep-the-memory / take-the-meaning distinction is the heart of the lore (canon rule 2)
and now sits alone in box 1 where it can't be skimmed past. The sundown line is her proof —
concrete, kitchen-scale — and closes the explanation with warmth, not mystery.*

**10. VESPER (thinking)**
- BEFORE: "(The kitchen-warm feeling, since the waystone. It isn't weather. It's coming out of the LAMPS.)"
- AFTER: "(That warm feeling, ever since the waystone. It isn't the weather. It's coming out of the LAMPS.)"

*Nearly kept — this parenthetical was already the right size: one thought, connecting the
lore she just heard to what she's felt since arriving. This is what internal lines are for.*

**11. VESPER (thinking)**
- BEFORE: "(I ate two. In my defense, they were extraordinary, and I have been walking for eleven days.)"
- AFTER: "(I ate two. In my defense: eleven days of walking, and they were extraordinary.)"

*Kept in spirit; the colon-list is her rhythm. Seeds the dishonest-honeybun-count runner.*

### A2 — Lake's visit (5 lines → 6 boxes)

**1. POPPY (happy)**
- BEFORE: "There he is! The only soul in Emberbrook allowed to be late tonight — because nothing starts till his lamps are lit. Bun?"
- AFTER: "There he is! The one soul allowed to be late tonight — nothing starts till his lamps are lit. Bun?"

*Trimmed; "nothing starts until the lamps are lit" is canon plain-knowledge and stays.*

**2. LAKE**
- BEFORE: "On duty, Poppy."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "On duty, Poppy."

*Three words. This is Lake. Every rewrite agent should tape this line to their monitor.*

**3. POPPY**
- BEFORE: "Half a bun. I'll hold the other half hostage until the Kindling Hour."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "Half a bun. I'll hold the other half hostage until the Kindling Hour."

**4. POPPY**
- BEFORE: "And no use asking if YOU'LL finally make a telling this year. Keeper keeps his own — I know, I know. Queerest rule your family ever kept. Saddest one, too."
- AFTER: "No use asking if YOU'LL make a telling this year. Keeper keeps his own — I know, I know. Strangest rule your family ever kept. Saddest one, too."

*"Keeper keeps his own" is tradition-register canon and survives verbatim — note Poppy
states it and does not explain it (STORY.md §7: villagers can't, and don't try).
"Queerest"→"Strangest" for the modern register.*

**5. LAKE (thinking)** → two boxes
- BEFORE: "(Everyone else banks their year in the flame. We keep ours the hard way. 'Someone has to stand outside the kept,' she said. I never asked why.)"
- AFTER (box 1): "(Everyone else gives their year to the flame, and the flame carries it for them. Keepers carry their own.)"
- AFTER (box 2): "('Someone has to stand outside the kept,' she said. I never asked why.)"

*The BEFORE was four sentences and two thoughts crammed into one parenthetical. Split at
the seam: box 1 is the plain fact, box 2 is the grandmother-quote — flagged as a quote,
which is Lake's licensed aphorism channel, and "I never asked why" seeds his whole arc.*

## SAMPLE B — Chapter 2: supper at the keepers' cottage
`chapter2.js`, `playSupper2`, all dialogue/narration steps in order (36 lines → 39 boxes).

*The scene's one earned aphorism is Odessa's* "You stopped standing still." *(line 12) —
it sits at the exact emotional peak (the doorframe beat, where the score cuts to silence),
it is four words, and it is her voice at full power: an enormous thing said as a flat
correction. Everything around it goes plain so it lands harder. Lake's "Kept a lamp. Same
wrist." (line 24) stays because it is trade-talk terseness, not a styled epigram — it's
Lake being plain, which is his voice, not a second aphorism. (The bag line — "The no was
what I owed my own heart. The bag is what I owed yours." — is Odessa's Chapter 2 aphorism
for the LANDING scene, `playLanding`, and budgets separately there.)*

**1. NARRATE**
- BEFORE: "The keepers' cottage held its heat the way the gorge held the town: close, and hard-won. Eel stew on the hook, bread in the window-iron, and the river talking quietly under the floorboards, off shift but never off duty."
- AFTER: "The keepers' cottage was small, warm, and hard-won. Eel stew on the hook, bread in the window-iron, and the river running quiet under the floorboards."

*One image per card. "Held its heat the way the gorge held the town" made the player do a
simile before they'd even seen the room; "off shift but never off duty" was a second
flourish on the same sentence. The river under the floorboards IS the image — it needs no
decoration.*

**2. ODESSA (grave)**
- BEFORE: "Wash. Basin's by the door. The cat eats on the step, and knows it."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "Wash. Basin's by the door. The cat eats on the step, and knows it."

*Already the target register: three rulings, nine words, zero explanation. Odessa was the
best-written voice in the old draft; her lines mostly survive.*

**3. MOCHI**
- BEFORE: "Mrrp."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "Mrrp."

**4. SYSTEM**
- BEFORE: "(Mochi eats on the step. On the WARM half of the step. The negotiation takes four seconds, and Odessa loses exactly half of it, and does not appear to mind the arithmetic.)"
- AFTER: "(Mochi eats on the step — the WARM half of the step. Odessa pretends not to notice.)"

*The joke is the warm half. The BEFORE explained the joke twice ("loses exactly half,"
"the arithmetic"). One beat, out.*

**5. SYSTEM**
- BEFORE: "(She serves the way she runs the locks: in order, without hurry, every bowl filled before her own is even down. She has not sat yet. It is not clear the chair expects her to.)"
- AFTER: "(Odessa serves everyone before herself — in order, without hurry, the way she runs the locks. She still hasn't sat down.)"

*Fact first, simile second. "It is not clear the chair expects her to" is exactly the
over-styled narrator tic the new direction retires: cute, slow, says nothing new.*

**6. MAREN (happy)** → two boxes
- BEFORE: "So — this is the house. Four generations. That beam's off a barge that sank in the '02 flood, Da reclaimed it — well, GRAND-da — and that's the good table. We're eating at the good table. Ma got out the good table."

- AFTER (box 2): "And that's the good table. We're eating at the good table. Ma got out the GOOD table."

*Her run-on energy and self-interruption are the voice — keep them, but give the reader a
box break to breathe. The triple "good table" is deliberate escalation and stays.*

**7. ODESSA (grave)**
- BEFORE: "The table we eat at."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "The table we eat at."

**8. MAREN**
- BEFORE: "The good one."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "The good one."

*This exchange is already the game at its best: seven words total, a whole
mother-daughter negotiation. Nothing to fix.*

**9. SYSTEM**
- BEFORE: "(By the door, climbing the frame: small dated marks. MAREN, and a height. MAREN, and a height. They rise like water coming up a lock wall — and stop, a hand below the lintel, years ago.)"
- AFTER: "(Small dated marks climb the doorframe. MAREN, and a height. MAREN, and a height. They stop years ago, a hand below the lintel.)"

*The repetition "MAREN, and a height" is the emotional engine — kept. The lock-wall simile
was a second engine bolted to the same cart — cut, so the stop lands on "stop."*

**10. LAKE**
- BEFORE: "The doorframe. Emberbrook does the same — Grandmother kept mine on the pantry door."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "The doorframe. Emberbrook does the same — Grandmother kept mine on the pantry door."

*Lake naming the obvious thing warmly, in one breath. Passes as-is.*

**11. MAREN**
- BEFORE: "Ma stopped measuring me at fourteen."
- AFTER: **(kept)** "Ma stopped measuring me at fourteen."

**12. ODESSA (grave) — THE SCENE'S APHORISM**
- BEFORE: "You stopped standing still."

*Four words at the music-out peak. This is the one line in the scene allowed to land
sideways — everything before and after is now plain enough to give it silence to ring in.*

**13. MAREN**

- AFTER: **(kept)** "You never asked me to."

**15. VESPER** → two boxes

## What these two samples demonstrate

1. **Plain first is mostly subtraction.** Of 47 before-lines, 21 ship unchanged and most
   others lose a clause, not their character. The beats, lore, and jokes all survive; the
   ornament between the player and the beat is what goes.
2. **One aphorism, placed.** Sample B keeps exactly one styled line ("You stopped standing
   still.") at the score's silence, and flattens the narrator's three competing flourishes
   around it. The peak now has the whole scene's style budget behind it.
3. **Voices survive the diet.** Poppy still legislates food, Vesper still files, Maren
   still runs on and doubles back, Odessa still rules in nine words, Lake still says the
   obvious true thing. Cover the nameplates in the AFTER column — the test still passes.
4. **Lore is untouched.** The Kindling Hour rewrite carries all four canon facts in canon
   vocabulary; "keeper keeps his own" and "stand outside the kept" survive verbatim,
   stated-not-explained, per STORY.md §7.
